2025 Delft station studio project

Start Point

To facilitate cross-track micro-sociality for waiting passengers at Delft Station through co-created, playful experiences.

Passengers waiting at Delft Station were isolated by their phones and headphones, turning waiting time into a disconnected and passive experience.

BingoTime turned that idle waiting into playful micro-social interaction between strangers.

(What Passengers thought about Delft Station)

Element Observation (The "Vacuum") Requirement (The Design Goal)
Situation Waiting: High time-anxiety & digital focus. Low-threshold: Quick-entry, zero-pressure play.
Interactions Zero: "Sensory Bubbling" (phones/music). Synchronicity: Break bubbles through shared focus.

The Cross-platform
Interaction Solution

Research showed that passengers wanted stimulation, but not direct social contact.The sandbox experiment revealed that distance across platforms created a safe social buffer, enabling connection through eye contact and synchronized actions.This led to a cross-platform interaction based on visual synchronization, designed to remain lightweight and non-intrusive to train awareness.

Low-fidelity prototyping testing at Delft station

Meanwhile, I selected three distinctive low-fidelity mechanisms for field testing:

The Bomb Game: Visually striking and with simple rules, it quickly captured the attention of many passersby.

Cross-platform Phone: Attempts to establish a direct voice connection.

Facing Time: Focuses on non-verbal action imitation.

Experiments showed that video and voice calls were less appealing. Participants reported that phone interactions created a certain "social burden," easily leading to the pressure of feeling trapped in small talk. Facing Time, on the other hand, lacked novelty for people.

The initial 'Bomb Game' prototype demonstrated higher engagement compared to voice-based interactions, confirming that competitive gamification effectively lowers the social threshold. However, mid-fidelity field testing revealed two critical usability flaws:

The game logic allowed participants to bypass direct competition, diminishing the intended social tension.

LOGO Design

Mushroom-head Button

Tactile Feedback: The large contact area increases the margin for error in reaction-based games, while the firm rebound enhances the sense of certainty at the moment of synchronization.

Dynamic Lighting: The button features built-in LED lighting effects, providing visual guidance upon hearing "Bingo" and offering immediate visual feedback for winning upon pressing.

Classic Red & Blue Palette: Classic red and blue colors for competition/cooperation. User testing showed this color scheme received the highest approval rating among respondents, demonstrating greater engagement and high recognizability compared to other colors.

Field Evaluation

Controlled Social Simulation(Campus/Library)

Sample: 17 participants (university students/faculty members), gender ratio balanced, aged 20-30 years.

Method: Quantitative questionnaire after use.

Purpose: To verify the system's usability, hardware stability, and interaction logic. Participants rated the game experience, ease of use, and willingness to replay.

Real-Scene Testing (Delft Train Station - Wizard of Oz Method)

Sample: 5 real commuters, gender ratio balanced, aged 20-30 years.

Method: Qualitative natural observation during the "Wizard of Oz" (WoZ) experiment.

Purpose: To measure the device's ability to create micro-interactions in a real-world context by observing nonverbal communication, particularly the duration of eye contact and the behavior of shifting gaze to the opposite platform.

Field Evaluation -questionnaire

This method, in addition to measuring a single level of liking, can simulate user emotions from two (or more) orthogonal dimensions:

Valence/Pleasure: Most users chose 3, indicating that this interaction evoked a positive emotional response.

Arousal: Most users chose 2 and 6, indicating that they experienced highly excited emotions rather than a calm sense of satisfaction.

This demonstrates that BingoTime creates a state of excitement with a competitive element. The combination of "high arousal + positive valence" creates gamified social interaction, making users not only find the interaction fun but also feel "energized" both physiologically and psychologically.
