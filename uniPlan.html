Start Point

How might we make academic guidance more accessible and supportive for college students with anxiety disorders to help them make confident decisions?

Scenario

Sofia is given a multi-step final project in her Desig n with AI Course, due next week, A cognitively deman ding, multi-step task Triggered her.

Sofia enters the task into UniPlan, selects the course, effort level, and deadline, UniPlan offers to break down the task but it is Sofia’s decision to make.

The next day, a new task arrives while Sofia is still working on the complex assignment. With no plan for handling both, her stress begins to rise.

Feeling overwhelmed, she starts avoiding the task. Her heart rate increases, which the UniBand detects silently.

Her wristband vibrates. Sofia glances at her phone where a UniPlan notification appears

Sofia chooses to re-plan. She selects ‘AI Suggestion’ to let UniPlan help her manage the tasks based on her preferences and deadlines.

UniPlan splits the complex task into smaller, manageable parts and reschedules them. It adds a lighter task from later in the week to maintain her flow without pressure.

She continues working—still feeling the pressure—but no longer overwhelmed. The plan feels realistic and under control.

The next morning, she submits the assignment and feels relieved. The process helped her stay on track—even under pressure.

College students with Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) face challenges in academic planning and decision-making confidence.

Difficulty in prioritizing tasks

Cognitive overload during anxiety

Negative emotions tied to deadlines and uncertainty

There is an opportunity to help these student's lack of managing abilities,

It is also challenging for counselors and schools to provide sufficient support to students while they are completing their regular academic tasks.
